# Chapter 1

## Segment 1

### Draft
Qin Liuxi saw this and was about to leave when the Old Lady called her back.

"Xidiao'er, stay and listen."

Liuxi paused in her steps, sat back down, and picked up a cup of tea.

**Chapter 14: The Malice of Small Men**

"Xidiao'er, when you were little, your health was poor, often falling ill due to the clash of energies in the Qin family. The old Daoist said your destiny was unusual, so we registered you under your birth mother early to suppress your fate with her noble aura, and then sent you to the ancestral home to live separately. This is why we returned you to the old house. Grandma knows you harbor resentment. You resent us for keeping you in the old house for ten years, but this was also for mutual benefit."

The Old Lady looked at Liuxi and said coolly, "Over these years, the Qin family hasn't stinted on your expenses, and the people we arranged for your care have been diligent. We've raised you to this day. The Qin family’s treatment of you isn’t without its coldness, but there’s also gratitude, for raising you."

Liuxi raised her eyes and met the Old Lady’s gaze, her expression calm and undisturbed.

The Old Lady’s words were accurate. Coldness and gratitude exist, the latter being the gratitude for raising her. However, there was a lack of companionship and nurturing. Yet, the preceding narrative had established a cause-and-effect relationship.

Liuxi stood up and bowed to the Old Lady.

The Old Lady, for some reason, felt even more stifled in her heart, bit her tongue, and continued, "You harbor resentment, Grandma understands. But you are surnamed Qin, and your name is registered in the Qin family’s genealogy as the eldest daughter of the main branch. As long as your name remains in the genealogy, you are still a Qin family daughter, the Young Lady."

"Grandfather and others have had their official titles revoked, how am I still the Young Lady?" Liuxi gave a self-deprecating smile.

The Old Lady bit her lip and said, "This doesn’t change our noble origins. I won’t beat around the bush. Our Qin family was stripped of our home and exiled by the Emperor. Do you know the details behind this?"

"I’d like to hear more."

The Old Lady closed her eyes, her face showing restraint.

Lady Wang then said, "Your grandfather was the Minister of Rites, a Third Rank official. During the July Temple Sacrifice, the sacrificial sheep suddenly died without warning, seen as inauspicious, leading to the Emperor’s fury..."

In a dynasty, the Temple Sacrifice is a major event, and the sacrificial rites are paramount. When such a crucial occasion sees a significant problem, naturally, it would anger the emperor, particularly the death of the sacrificial sheep.

In the founding of the Great Hong Dynasty, it was said that the founding emperor was surrounded by enemies and led his troops into a jungle, losing their way for nearly two weeks. Running out of supplies, a sheep appeared out of nowhere, leading them through the mist, allowing them to find their way out and reunite with reinforcements. This victory established the Great Hong Dynasty.

Thus, the founding emperor revered the sheep as a divine creature, and it became a symbol of good fortune for the Great Hong. During the Temple Sacrifice, there must be a live sheep.

But now, the sacrificial sheep had suddenly died, blood gushing from its seven apertures, considered inauspicious and disrespectful, even hinting at the emperor’s lack of virtue.

When the emperor is angry, hundreds of thousands perish.

Qin Yuanshan, the Minister of Rites, failed to oversee properly, and with the interference of the Queen Consort Meng, who despised the Qin family, the accusation of disrespect and disloyalty fell heavily on the Qin family, who could not resist and immediately disintegrated.

When Lady Wang spoke to this point, she clenched her fist, her lips pursed.

Liuxi said, "In such an important event, how could Grandfather be careless? There must be small men at work!"

The Old Lady was surprised by her sharp insight, grinding her teeth in hatred, "You are right. Your grandfather is cautious by nature, and in such a crucial event, he wouldn’t dare to be lax. But no matter how careful, he was still deceived!"

**Chapter 15: Who Designed the Qin Family's Downfall**

At the height of her agitation, the Old Lady coughed violently, her head spinning. Lady Wang and Nurse Ding quickly attended to her.

After a while, the Old Lady’s breathing eased, but her face was even paler.

## Segment 2

### Draft
Qi Liuxi glanced at the situation and said, "Setting aside whether grandfather was wronged, the court is like walking on thin ice; when the emperor wants someone to die, he must die, even if he is in high favor with the emperor, a mere pretext could lead to his downfall. Since things have come to this point, worrying won’t help. You need to take care of your health first."

Her detached and calm tone not only tightened the frown on the Old Lady’s brow but also left even Lady Wang and the others feeling rather complicated.

This young lady, though registered under her name, had been raised in the old estate from a young age without the guidance of elders. It was impressive that she could speak with such composure.

Having the emperor’s favor is good, but sometimes it’s like a knife hanging above one’s head, ready to fall at any moment.

Among the younger generation of the family, all were still in shock and fear over the family’s downfall and the loss of their wealth and glory. None were as composed as Qi Liuxi, who could speak of the dangers of court life with such ease.

Lady Wang felt a strong sense of curiosity towards this young lady registered under her name.

Her composure and expertise in medicine—over the past decade, who had been teaching her? Had it been that Red Yuan Daoist?

"Even though everything is set in stone, we must still bring your grandfather, father, uncles, and younger brother back," the Old Lady said resolutely. "I won’t be at ease until I see the exoneration of my family, until I see my male relatives return from that harsh northwest region, even if it means I can’t rest in peace, cough, cough..."

Qi Liuxi responded, "Then you really need to take care of yourself."

After all, her life expectancy was not long.

The Old Lady was visibly upset, her face turning dark.

Lady Xie said, "Are you intentionally trying to upset your grandmother?"

Qi Liuxi silently picked up her tea and took a sip, not responding.

Lady Xie felt as though she had hit a soft pillow, her frustration evident.

The Old Lady raised her hand and continued, "The suddenness of the confiscation of our home and the prohibition from taking any belongings was due to the head of the confiscation team being Meng Li from the Meng family."

Qi Liuxi looked at Lady Wang, as if waiting for an explanation.

Lady Wang then said, "Three years ago, the Meng family sent a woman to the palace who received great favor, rising to the rank of Consort within three years. Our family has never been on good terms with the Meng family. Even when your grandfather spoke of the Meng family’s fox-like charm and how their virtue did not match their position after drinking with friends, it was recorded and held against us to this day..."
