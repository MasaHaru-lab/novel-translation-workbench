Madam Qin began coughing violently as she grew agitated, her head spinning in waves. Madam Wang and Nanny Ding hurried forward to pat her back and soothe her.

It took a while for Madam Qin’s breathing to ease, but her face turned even paler.

Qin Liuxi glanced over and said, “Let’s set aside whether Grandfather was wronged or not. In the imperial court, serving the emperor is like living with a tiger—if the sovereign wants a minister dead, the minister has no choice but to die. Even those who enjoy the emperor’s deepest favor can be dismissed on the flimsiest pretext once His Majesty decides to act. What’s done is done. Dwelling on it does no good; you must preserve your health first.”

Her calm, almost detached narration not only made Madam Qin frown deeply, but even Madam Wang and the others felt complicated emotions stir.

This girl, though registered under Madam Wang’s name, had been fostered at the old family estate since childhood, without elders by her side to guide her over the years. It was remarkable she could articulate such insights.

The emperor’s favor was a good thing, yet sometimes it was also a blade hanging overhead, never knowing when it might fall.

The younger generation of the family were still reeling from the Qin family’s downfall, mourning the loss of wealth and glory. None of them could speak of “serving the emperor is like living with a tiger” the way Qin Liuxi did.

Madam Wang grew intensely curious about this girl registered under her name.

Her composure, her medical skills—who had been teaching her these past ten years? That old Daoist priest, Chiyuan?

“Even though the matter is settled, we must bring back your grandfather, your father, your uncles, and your younger brother,” Madam Qin said fiercely. “If I don’t see the Qin family cleared of wrongdoing, if I don’t see our men return from that bitter cold northwest frontier, I… I won’t be able to close my eyes even in death, cough…”

Qin Liuxi replied, “Then you really must take care of your health.”

After all, her remaining years were not long.

Madam Qin choked, her face darkening.

Madam Xie said, “You little brat, are you deliberately trying to anger your grandmother?”

Qin Liuxi silently picked up her teacup and took a sip, offering no reply.

Madam Xie felt as though she’d punched cotton—frustrated and deflated.

Madam Qin raised her hand and continued, “The confiscation came suddenly, and we weren’t allowed to hide any valuables. That’s because the officer leading the raid was Meng Li of the Meng family.”

Qin Liuxi looked at Madam Wang, waiting for an explanation.

Madam Wang explained, “Three years ago, the Meng family sent a woman into the palace. She gained great favor and, in just three years, rose to become an Imperial Noble Consort. Our family has never gotten along with the Mengs. Even when Consort Meng was promoted, your grandfather once said to friends after drinking that ‘the Meng woman bewitches with her charms; her virtue doesn’t match her position.’ Those words have been held against us ever since…”
