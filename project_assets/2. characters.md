# Characters

## Confirmed / working renderings

### Qin Liuxi
- Chinese: 秦流西
- English rendering: Qin Liuxi
- Notes: Main character. Do not drift to "Qi Liuxi" or other variants.

**Address forms for 秦流西:**

| Context | Chinese form | English rendering |
|---|---|---|
| Full name (narrative / formal) | 秦流西 | Qin Liuxi |
| Familiar address (by elders, affection) | 西丫头, 丫头, 你这丫头 | Xi'er |
| Generic noun reference (narrative, 3rd-party observer) | 这个姑娘 / 她 | this young woman / she (contextual; prefers neutral form unless register demands closer familiarity) |

- Use "Qin Liuxi" as the narrative default. Reserve "Xi'er" for familiar direct address (elder speaking to her). Do not over-use "Xi'er" in narrative voice or from characters who would not use familiar address.
- Generic references (这个姑娘, 那姑娘, 她 as "the young woman") are deliberately neutral — do not replace them with "Xi'er" or "Qin Liuxi" unless the context clearly calls for named reference. The generic form is a valid register choice in the source.

### Chi Yuan Daoist
- Chinese: 赤元老道
- English rendering: Chi Yuan Daoist
- Notes: Canonical rendering. 赤=chi=red, rendered as pinyin here for name-like flow. Prefer this over "Red Yuan Daoist" (too descriptive) or "Daoist Red Yuan" (over-forced).
